# Board Briefing: MedDefense Security Posture

**MedDefense’s security posture is critically vulnerable.** Fundamental gaps expose patient data and care delivery to immediate, confirmed cyberattack. We are currently hosting an active intruder on our network who could escalate to ransomware at any moment.

**Our network lacks segmentation; a hacker reaching one device can control all systems, including life-critical infusion pumps.** Three recent regional hospital breaches followed this exact pattern, causing millions in losses, extended operational shutdowns, and direct patient harm.

**Priority Actions:**
1. Contain active compromise on billing server immediately ($3,500, Week 1).
2. Implement network segmentation to isolate clinical and financial systems ($8,000, Month 1).
3. Deploy centralized monitoring to detect attacks before damage occurs ($10,000, Month 2).

**These foundational steps require only a small fraction of the requested $120,000 annual security budget.** That total investment prevents costs exceeding $5 million, the average total cost including fines and lost revenue similar regional healthcare organizations reported last year. This spending protects patient safety, ensures strict regulatory compliance, and secures our reputation against irreversible destruction. We must treat this not as a discretionary IT expense but as essential insurance against existential corporate risk.

**Failure to approve these resources leaves the organization exposed to catastrophic financial liability, operational paralysis, and potential leadership turnover.**
